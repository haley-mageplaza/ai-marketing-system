# Shopplaza ASO Rules (Shopify App Store Optimization)

## 1. ASO Overview

Shopplaza ASO focuses on TWO systems:

### A. App Store Optimization (Primary)
- Ranking within Shopify App Store
- Improving conversion rate (views → installs)

### B. SEO → ASO Bridge (Support)
- Blog drives traffic
- Content builds intent
- Traffic converts into installs

👉 ASO is NOT just keywords — it is conversion + positioning

---

## 2. ASO Goals

Each app listing must:
- Rank for relevant keywords
- Clearly communicate value
- Convert visitors into installs
- Reduce friction in decision-making

---

## 3. App Positioning Framework

Every app must clearly answer:

1. What problem does this solve?
2. Who is it for? (B2B or B2C)
3. What outcome does it deliver?
4. Why is it better than alternatives?

---

### Positioning Examples

#### Weak:
- "Request a quote app for Shopify"

#### Strong:
- "Let customers request quotes and negotiate pricing so you can close high-value B2B deals faster"

---

## 4. Listing Structure (Mandatory)

Each app listing must follow:

### 1. Title
- Include primary keyword
- Clear and direct

Example:
- "Request a Quote, Hide Price for B2B"

---

### 2. Subtitle (Short Description)
- Outcome-driven
- Focus on benefit

Example:
- "Let customers request quotes, hide prices, and manage B2B orders easily"

---

### 3. First Paragraph (Critical)
- Must hook immediately
- Must define problem + solution

---

### 4. Feature Sections
Each feature must:
- Start with benefit
- Explain how it works
- Include outcome

---

### 5. Use Cases
- Real merchant scenarios
- B2B vs B2C clarity

---

### 6. Social Proof
- Ratings
- Reviews
- Experience

---

### 7. CTA
👉 Install / Try free

---

## 5. Keyword Strategy (ASO)

Each app must target:

- Primary keyword
- Feature keywords
- Problem-based keywords

---

### Example (Request a Quote app)

Primary:
- request a quote shopify

Secondary:
- shopify quote app
- b2b shopify pricing
- hide price shopify

---

## 6. Keyword Placement

Keywords should appear in:
- Title
- Subtitle
- First paragraph
- Feature headings
- Naturally throughout content

---

## 7. Conversion Optimization (Most Important)

ASO success depends heavily on conversion.

### Must optimize:

#### Clarity
- Users understand value in 5 seconds

#### Relevance
- Matches their problem

#### Trust
- Reviews, ratings

#### Simplicity
- No complex explanation

---

## 8. Feature Writing Rules

Each feature must follow:

👉 Benefit → How → Outcome

---

### Example

❌ Weak:
- "Hide price feature"

✅ Strong:
- "Hide prices from guests or specific customers so you can control who sees your pricing and protect your margins"

---

## 9. Screenshots & Visual Rules

Each screenshot must:
- Focus on ONE message
- Highlight benefit, not UI only
- Include short text (clear and bold)

---

### Screenshot structure:

- Headline (benefit)
- Supporting line (how)
- Visual (UI)

---

### Example:

- "Hide prices for non-logged users"
- "Control who sees your pricing"

---

## 10. B2B vs B2C ASO Strategy

### B2C Apps
Focus:
- Conversion
- UX
- Sales growth

Messaging:
- Increase AOV
- Boost engagement
- Improve shopping experience

---

### B2B Apps
Focus:
- Workflow
- Pricing control
- Operations

Messaging:
- Manage wholesale customers
- Control pricing visibility
- Automate quote process

---

## 11. Review Optimization

Encourage reviews that mention:
- Use cases
- Outcomes
- Ease of use

---

## 12. Competitive Positioning

Every listing must:
- Be clearer than competitors
- Be more outcome-driven
- Avoid generic phrases

---

## 13. Common ASO Mistakes

- Feature dumping
- No clear positioning
- Weak first paragraph
- No use cases
- Generic language
- Overloaded screenshots

---

## 14. SEO → ASO Bridge

Blog content must:
- Reinforce app keywords
- Introduce use cases
- Link to app listing

---

## 15. ASO Checklist

Before publishing:

- Clear problem statement
- Strong first paragraph
- Keywords included
- Features benefit-driven
- Screenshots optimized
- CTA clear
- Internal linking ready

---

## 16. Key Principle

👉 ASO is about:

Not:
- "What the app does"

But:
- "What the merchant achieves"
